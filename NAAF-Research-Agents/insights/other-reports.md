# Insights from Other Reports (2023–2025)

This file captures additional signals that can strengthen the overall research. These reports are **not** the core framework and are kept separate from the Eight Layers model.

## 1) Stanford AI Index Report 2024 (AI Index, Stanford)
**Why it matters:** A global snapshot of AI development, investment, and policy trends.

**Key insights to incorporate:**
- The report shows industry continues to dominate notable model production.
- Private investment in generative AI surged, indicating strong commercialization momentum.
- AI regulation activity is increasing across countries, signaling faster governance activity.

**How to use:**
- Use AI Index trends as global baselines.
- Use policy signals to flag governance momentum.

**Source:** https://aiindex.stanford.edu/report/

## 2) IMF AI Preparedness Index (AIPI) 2023 (International Monetary Fund)
**Why it matters:** A cross‑country readiness index that complements the 8‑layer model.

**Core dimensions:**
- **Digital Infrastructure**
- **Human Capital & Labor Market Policies**
- **Technological Innovation & Integration**
- **Legal Frameworks**

**How to use:**
- Treat AIPI as a baseline readiness score and compare with other sources.

**Source:** https://www.imf.org/en/Publications/Staff-Discussion-Notes/Issues/2023/11/14/AI-Preparedness-Index-539627

## 3) OECD AI Principles — State of Implementation (OECD)
**Why it matters:** Tracks how countries are implementing the OECD AI recommendations and catalogs national policies.

**Five OECD Recommendations (implementation lens):**
- Invest in AI R&D
- Foster a digital ecosystem
- Shape an enabling policy environment
- Build human capacity and prepare for labor market transformation
- Foster international cooperation

**How to use:**
- Apply these as policy maturity checks.
- Use the OECD AI Policy database to validate national AI strategies and initiatives.

**Source:** https://www.oecd-ilibrary.org/science-and-technology/implementing-the-oecd-ai-principles_129e4de6-en

## 4) OECD.AI Policy Observatory (OECD)
**Why it matters:** An online platform with policy instruments, national strategies, and implementation tools for trustworthy AI.

**How to use:**
- Use OECD.AI as a **primary source registry** for national AI strategies and policy instruments.
- Cross‑check for policy updates that affect Layer 1 and Layer 4.

**Source:** https://oecd.ai/en/
